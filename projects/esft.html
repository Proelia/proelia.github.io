Emergent Spacetime Flux Theory (ESFT)

A computational framework designed to model universal expansion and gravitational effects as emergent properties of spacetime flux, rather than relying on Dark Energy or the Cosmological Constant.

Core Theoretical Framework

ESFT re-conceptualizes gravity as the local flow rate of a quantum flux medium and time as the local energy gradient. Accelerated cosmic expansion is modeled as a "swelling" of the flux driven by energy dissipation from microscopic gravitational eddies.

// Fundamental Flux Tensors
Gravity (g) ≡ local flow rate (v)
Time (t) ≡ local energy gradient (∂ρ/∂t)

Computational Methodology

Implemented a Bayesian MCMC Framework using the emcee Python library to estimate cosmological parameters. The model utilizes the Pantheon+ SN Ia dataset's full N×N covariance matrix to account for complex systematic uncertainties.

Primary Datasets
  • Pantheon+ (Supernovae)
  • Planck 2018/2020 (CMB)
  • SDSS DR16 (BAO)
Model Selection

Utilizes Akaike Information Criterion (AIC) for rigorous comparison against ΛCDM. Current results yield ΔAIC ≈ 0, establishing a baseline for unique redshift dependency refinements.

Peer Rebuttal & Validation

Authored formal scientific responses addressing reviewer critiques on galactic disk conventions and holographic scaling. Numerical simulations demonstrated a statistically significant fit improvement for anomalous rotation curves using a "Flux Wake" model.
